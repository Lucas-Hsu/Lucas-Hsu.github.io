Frabjous Fuzzk - Devlog #2

Game Theme

I'm going to design the game from a top down approach. This will allow me to constantly keep track of my goals and the direction I want to design my game towards. All the features I add into the game should adhere to the core experience I want the game to provide.

Last night I had a dream about a blob creature with a smiley face bobbing up and down like it's dancing, but suddenly attacking a bird flying nearby. It might be a coincidence, but that's exactly what I want for my game, as it fits into the difficulty and fun design I set down a few days ago.

The core experience of the game will be:
"You are a playful blob creature that enjoys living with your brothers on the grass plain. But occasionally, when you are hungry, you take the role of a violent and ruthless predator, hunting down your prey."

The new gameplay loop flowchart is shown in the diagram below. The high-intensity gameplay loop still obeys the Challenge kind of fun, but the low-intensity loop does not entirely fit into the Submission kind of fun. In a way, I mixed Fellowship into the game. And this is actually very good, as the reason why I wanted to include a mix of difficult and easy gameplay was to make sure players don't give up too soon. Socializing with NPCs within the game would be a good way of keeping the player engaged even if they failed to capture prey that day.

Precise, timed controls and computation challenges will be neccessary to successfully hunt and kill a prey. For example, maintaining a safe distance away from prey to prevent from alerting them, throwing rocks along a correct trajectory to stun the prey, and closing in for a kill. Thinking about it right now, I think it will add another layer of difficulty if the prey has a hard shell that requires the player to find weakpoints and inject toxins into the prey to kill it before it runs away. On the other hand, navigational skills are neccessary for locating the brother blobs and returning home to sleep for the night.

Game Name

Now that we have the core experience of the game set down, I can come up with a name that encompasses that idea, so that players will be able to see what the game is about right from the name.

If you didn't notice already, this devlog's background music is from an Alice through the Looking Glass film. I like how Lewis Caroll creates imaginary words by combining two different adjectives. The Jabberwocky poem especially creates a playful and childish tone while also depicting violence, which fits my game perfectly.

The player character is fluffy, fuzzy, cute, but also spiky, quick, and dangerous. Maybe, a kitten and a spider. For me, 'K' is a very sharp letter, so I just put that onto a word like fuzzy, and I get: "Fuzzk". I think it's pretty good, because when you read it, you expect it to say fuzzy, like you would expect the player character to be fuzzy and cute like usual, but it is unexpectedly sharp and dangerous.

Frabjous Fuzzk

I think that sounds very nice and is pretty unique. Better than just naming it "PlayHunt Game" like I would if I used my old game naming convention. >v<

Game Tools

Game Engine: I'm going to use the Godot Engine for this game. I've used Godot before, and felt that it was fun to use, compared to Unity which felt very serious. Not only that, there are several features that Godot provides better than Unity, such as the intuitive node and scene based system, or making 2D-only games instead of 2D as an overlay on a 3D world. More importantly GDScript is an interpreted language with optional static typing, which would make it quick and easy for me to code and debug the game. Godot has an integrated code editor, so I will be using that.

Art Program: I'm not skilled at drawing, so I'm just going to choose an app that makes it easy to create animations for the game. Pencil2D is free and has all the features I need for drawing simple art and animations with no shading.

DAW: I'm going to use Logic Pro because I've always used it, and it has lots of features. Music is the lowest priority though. I'm probably just going to add some ambient sound effects.

Success Criteria

I used to just add features whenever I wanted as I programmed the game, but that made development time drag on way past the day I thought it would be done, since I always wanted to add new things. The list I'm making will be how I make sure I have compeleted everything I wanted to do and am ready to release a version 1 of the game. I can make improvements and additions after I release it based on feedback, of course.

To motivate the player and give the gameplay a sense of urgency, I will add a revenge story. Some scary monster ate the Red Fuzzk, your best friend, so now you have to call the other Fuzzks to fight back and recover Red Fuzzk from scary monster's stomach. You have to eat lots of Shells to level up so that you are strong enough to beat the monster.

  • Player actions (Overworld)
    1. Move around in 8 directions (because keyboard)
    2. Kick in the direction of cursor, knocking items away
    3. Dance in 4 directions when not moving
    4. Interact with other Fuzzks to recruit them if you have enough stamina to dance with them
    5. Pick up items and throw them to the direction of cursor
    6. Interact with stunned Shells to devour them (enter hunt mode)
    7. Leveling up increases max stamina
    8. Sleep to replenish stamina
  • NPCs
    1. 6 Other Fuzzks (ur brothers), each a different color of the rainbow
    2. Shells, which Fuzzks prey on, but have hard shells.
    3. Mosquitoes that fly around and sting you unless you kick them.
    4. Scary Monster in the cave.
  • Player actions (Hunt Mode)
    1. Sweep the enemy's body for weak points
    2. Insert each of your feet into into the weak points
    3. Inject venom, which is a minigame like Space Invaders, but in the shape of living cells
    4. If the enemy does not die, you exit hunt mode and have to kick the enemy to stun them before they escape
    5. You can flee by not kicking the enemy and running out of range of the enemy
  • Game Objectives
    1. If you run out of stamina, you lose some max stamina and respawn, which means some of the Fuzzks might leave you until you beat them at dancing again
    2. You need all 5 Fuzzks on your team to make the scary monster bossfight not ridiculously difficult.
    3. If you beat the scary monster, you win.
    4. After that, you can just run around doing whatever you want with you 6 Fuzzk brothers.
  • Feature Checklist
    1. Stamina system
    2. Persistent world map of locations of Fuzzks and Scary Monster
    3. Shell and Mosquito spawning/despawning system
    4. Random rocks spawning
  • Bonus (Not necessary and complicated to implement, but would be fun)
    1. Grass and flowers
    2. Non-essential sound effects and music
    3. Particle effects, wind, and other graphical details
    4. Rivers and jumping across rivers
    5. Day and nights / time system, so that the world feels more alive. Prey is asleep at night, but visibility is lower
    6. Speech system for Fuzzks, so they can have different personalities

All of the features I included above fit into either the categor of fun experience of living as a cute creature, or the category of hunting animals with precise controls and complex steps. However, I am suprised at how much stuff I have to implement. Therefore, my development plan will have to prioritize the most important things first.
